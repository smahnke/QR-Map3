Introduction.

In The New Normal, Michael Warner discusses the zoning laws of New York that pushed out the queer public sphere in favor of market capital space. As Warner suggests, te erasure of queer spaces and places is symptom of societal regulation of sex through the delineation of the public/private (161). Time Square, for instance, has demonstrated a cleansing of the commercial space, resulting in the removal of queer clubs, porn shops, adult stores, etc. Though Warner explores the various spaces of New York that have been affected, it is symptomatic of a nation-wide push to prioritize market capital over the creation of a democratic public sphere.
